Beginner's Guide

The Basics

Each run, you pick two clans: a Primary clan (gives you your Champion and most of your core cards) and a Secondary clan (adds support cards and extra synergy options).

At the top of your train sits the Pyre — your core objective. If it's destroyed, the run ends. Certain unlockable Pyres grant passive bonuses that change how a run plays out.

Covenant 0 vs Covenant 1 — which should I play?

Short answer: start on Covenant 0, but move to Covenant 1 quickly — it seeds your deck with extra cards and a free unit, which makes runs more varied and actually easier to learn from, not harder.
  • Covenant 0 — the gentlest starting point, good for your very first runs.
  • Covenant 1 — slightly tougher enemies, but the added cards make it worth it once you've won a run or two.
  • Only raise the Covenant level once you're consistently finishing runs at your current one — there's no rush.

Losing floor 1 by turn 3? Fix your positioning first

If your run keeps collapsing early, it's almost always a positioning problem before it's a card problem. Check this first, before you touch your deck.

Combat happens across multiple floors of your train, and where you place units matters:

  • Put your tankiest unit (highest HP/armor) in the front of each floor — it absorbs incoming attacks.
  • Put fragile, high-damage units behind them.
  • Your Champion is strongest held in reserve on a back floor, building up power before committing.

Why do my runs fall apart mid-game?

Usually one of these four. Check the list below before assuming your deck is just unlucky.
  • Overfilling every floor with units just because you can.
  • Ignoring upcoming enemy previews/abilities before a fight.
  • Keeping every starter card "just in case" instead of trimming your deck.
  • Treating optional trial challenges as mandatory — skipping a risky one is often correct early on.

Best Starting Clan

Short answer: pick The Banished. It's the most forgiving clan to learn the game's core mechanics on.

Why The Banished works for new players

Their playstyle centers on movement — many of their units use a "Shift" mechanic that triggers a bonus effect whenever a unit moves position, plus a "Valor" stacking buff that boosts attack and armor over time.

Their Champion can become a genuine run-ender once buffed — held in reserve on a back floor stacking Valor, then unleashed as a multi-hit finisher capable of clearing an entire wave in one go.

Good Secondary Pairings

  • A clan with strong frontline tank units pairs naturally with Banished's more mobile playstyle.
  • A spell-focused clan is a solid alternate pairing if your primary clan already has enough dedicated fighters.

FAQ

Which Covenant level should I start on?

Start at Covenant 0 or 1. Stay there until you're winning consistently before raising the difficulty.

What happens if my Pyre is destroyed?

The run ends immediately. Protecting it is the actual win condition, not just surviving each individual fight.

How does card rarity work?

Common cards form the foundation of your strategy and usually reflect a clan's core keywords. Higher rarities build on that foundation rather than replacing it.

I keep losing — what's the most common mistake?

Spreading your deck too thin across too many mechanics instead of committing to one clear strategy early.
